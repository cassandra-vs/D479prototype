Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know for a smooth visit

Click on any section below to expand and view the answers

💰 Money & Banking

▼
Q: What currency is used on the island?
A: The island uses the U.S. Dollar (USD) as its official currency. Many businesses also accept Euros (EUR) and Japanese Yen (JPY), though you may receive change in USD. Credit cards are widely accepted at hotels, restaurants, and larger shops.
Q: Where can I exchange money?
A: Several banks in Island City offer currency exchange services during business hours (typically 9am-4pm weekdays). The airport also has a currency exchange kiosk. Most ATMs dispense USD and accept international cards.
Q: Are credit cards accepted everywhere?
A: Major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express) are accepted at most hotels, restaurants, and tour operators. However, some small cafés, markets, and street vendors may be cash only. It's wise to carry some cash for small purchases.
Q: What about tipping customs?
A: Tipping is customary and appreciated on the island. Standard guidelines:
  • Restaurants: 15-20% for good service
  • Taxis: 10-15% of fare
  • Hotel staff: $2-5 per day for housekeeping, $1-2 per bag for porters
  • Tour guides: $10-20 per person for full-day tours

🏥 Safety & Health

▼
Q: Is the island safe for tourists?
A: Yes, the island is generally very safe for tourists. Violent crime is rare. However, as tourism has increased, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas. Take normal precautions: don't leave valuables unattended on the beach, be aware of your surroundings in crowded areas, and secure valuables in your hotel safe.
Q: What medical facilities are available?
A: The island has one hospital and several clinics with modern medical equipment. The hospital staff includes multilingual employees who can communicate in English and other languages. For minor issues, clinics are located throughout Island City and Merriton Landing. Travel insurance with medical coverage is recommended.
Q: Do I need any vaccinations?
A: No special vaccinations are required for the island. However, it's recommended to be up-to-date on routine vaccinations (measles, mumps, rubella, etc.). Consult your doctor or a travel medicine clinic before your trip for personalized advice.
Q: Is the tap water safe to drink?
A: Tap water in Island City and major tourist areas is treated and generally safe to drink. However, many visitors prefer bottled water, which is widely available. If you have a sensitive stomach, stick to bottled water, especially when visiting rural areas.

🚗 Getting Around

▼
Q: Do I need an international driver's license?
A: An International Driving Permit (IDP) is recommended but not required for visitors from the US and Canada. Your valid driver's license from home is legally acceptable, but an IDP can be helpful if stopped by police. Visitors from other countries should check requirements with the rental agency.
Q: Is it easy to get around without a car?
A: It depends on your plans. If you're staying in Island City or Yellow Leaf Bay and mainly want beach/city experiences, you can manage with public buses, taxis, and walking. However, if you want to explore the volcano, rainforest, and entire island, a rental car is highly recommended for flexibility and convenience.

🌺 Local Customs & Etiquette

▼
Q: What language is spoken?
A: The island has its own local language. However, many younger islanders speak fluent English, especially in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. In rural areas and among older residents, very little English is spoken. Learning a few basic phrases in the local language is appreciated and helpful.
Q: What should I wear?
A: The island has a relaxed, casual atmosphere. Lightweight, breathable clothing is best due to the tropical climate. For beaches and casual dining, shorts and t-shirts are fine. Some upscale restaurants may prefer "resort casual" (no swimwear, but shorts and nice shirts acceptable). When visiting cultural sites or rural areas, modest clothing (covering shoulders and knees) is respectful.
Q: Are there any cultural customs I should know?
A: Islanders are generally friendly and welcoming to visitors. Basic courtesy goes a long way:
  • Greet people with a smile and "hello"
  • Ask permission before photographing locals
  • Respect "no entry" signs at private or sacred areas
  • Remove shoes when entering homes (if invited)
  • Be mindful of noise levels, especially near residential areas
Q: What are the alcohol regulations?
A: Alcohol sales and service are prohibited between midnight and 9:00 AM. The drinking age is 18 years old, though enforcement is not strict. Many restaurants, bars, and pubs serve alcohol during permitted hours. Please drink responsibly.

ℹ️ General Information

▼
Q: What type of power outlets are used?
A: The island uses 120-volt electrical outlets, the same as in the United States. If you're traveling from a country that uses different voltage (like Europe or Asia), you'll need a voltage converter and/or plug adapter for your devices.
Q: What about national holidays?
A: The island observes many national holidays throughout the year. During these holidays, many tourist attractions, restaurants, and shops may be closed. It's important to check the holiday calendar when planning your visit and make reservations accordingly. Your hotel can provide information about upcoming holidays during your stay.
Q: What's the weather like?
A: The island enjoys a tropical climate year-round with temperatures typically ranging from 75-85°F (24-29°C). There's no distinct dry or wet season, though brief rain showers can occur any time. Pack light, breathable clothing, sunscreen, and a light rain jacket. Mornings often have the clearest weather.
Q: Is WiFi readily available?
A: Free WiFi is standard in most hotels, many restaurants, and cafés. Cell phone coverage is good in populated areas (Island City, Merriton Landing) but may be limited in mountainous or remote areas. International roaming can be expensive - consider purchasing a local SIM card or using WiFi calling apps.
Q: What should I pack?
A: Essential items for your island visit:
  • Sunscreen (high SPF) and sunglasses
  • Light, breathable clothing and swimwear
  • Comfortable walking shoes and water shoes for rocky beaches
  • Light rain jacket or umbrella
  • Insect repellent (for rainforest visits)
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Camera and chargers
  • Basic first aid kit and any prescription medications
Q: Can I use my mobile phone?
A: Yes, most international mobile phones work on the island. Check with your carrier about international roaming rates, which can be expensive. Many visitors use WiFi calling apps (WhatsApp, Skype) when connected to WiFi to avoid roaming charges. Local SIM cards are available for purchase if you have an unlocked phone.
