Getting Here & Around

Everything you need to know about island transportation

Getting to the Island

✈️ By Air (Primary)

Most visitors arrive by air

Airport: Island International Airport

Current Capacity: Small jets and propeller planes

Expansion: Airport expansion in progress to accommodate larger jets within the next few years

Location: 10 minutes from Island City

Main Entry Point Daily Flights

🚢 By Cruise Ship

Limited service available

Schedule: One small cruise ship docks per week

Location: Yellow Leaf Bay harbor

Duration: One night port stop

Note: Limited time to explore the island

Weekly Service Harbor Docking

Getting Around the Island

Multiple transportation options available to suit your needs and budget.

Public Transportation

🚌 Public Buses - Island City

$ (Budget-Friendly) - $2-4/ride

📍 Service Area: Island City only 🕐 Hours: 5am - 11pm daily

Regular bus service throughout Island City and surrounding areas. Routes cover main tourist areas, beaches, and downtown. Buses run every 20-30 minutes.

Payment: Cash only (exact change preferred)

Frequent Service Beach Access

🚐 Private Buses

$$ - $5-15/ride

📍 Service Area: Rest of island 🕐 Hours: Vary by route

Private bus services connect Island City to other parts of the island including rainforest area, volcano viewpoints, and rural areas. Less frequent than city buses.

Tip: Check schedules at your hotel or tourist information center

Island-Wide Scenic Routes

Private Transportation Options

🚕 Taxis

$$ - $10-30 typical fare

📍 Available in Island City 🕐 24/7 availability

Licensed taxis available throughout Island City. Can be hailed on street, called by phone, or found at taxi stands near hotels and popular areas.

Fares: Metered rates - approximately $3 base + $2/mile

Tip: Tipping 10-15% is customary

Metered Credit Cards Accepted

🚗 Rental Cars - RECOMMENDED

$$ - $40-70/day

📍 Location: Near airport 🕐 Office Hours: 7am - 7pm daily

Local rental agency offers compact to SUV vehicles. Best option for flexibility and exploring the entire island at your own pace.

Requirements:

  • Valid driver's license from home country
  • International Driving Permit (IDP) recommended but not required for US/Canadian licenses
  • Minimum age: 21 years (25 for some vehicle types)
  • Credit card for deposit

Insurance: Available at rental counter. Check if your credit card provides coverage.

Fuel: Gasoline stations in Island City and Merriton Landing. Prices similar to US mainland.

Most Flexible Book Ahead Popular Choice

🤝 Car Share / Ride Share

$$ - Varies by distance

📍 Island City area 🕐 Limited availability

Limited ride-sharing services available through mobile apps. Coverage mainly in Island City and Merriton Landing areas.

Note: Service may be limited during peak hours or in remote areas

App-Based Limited Coverage

Alternative Transportation

🚌 Hotel Shuttles

$ - Free to $10

Many hotels offer complimentary or low-cost shuttle service to airport, beaches, and shopping areas. Check with your hotel about schedules and destinations.

Hotel Guests Only Limited Routes

🚴 Bike Rentals

$ - $15-25/day

📍 Multiple vendors 🕐 Varies by vendor

Bicycles available for rent from several vendors in Island City. Great for exploring flat areas and beachfront.

IMPORTANT: Helmets required by law and included with rental

Best For: Island City center, beach areas, short distances

Helmet Included Eco-Friendly

🚶 Walking

Free

Island City and Merriton Landing are both very walkable with flat terrain and pedestrian-friendly streets. Many attractions, restaurants, and shops within walking distance of hotels.

Best Areas for Walking:

  • Island City center - compact and flat
  • Merriton Landing - entertainment district
  • Yellow Leaf Bay waterfront
Highly Walkable Scenic

💡 Transportation Tips & Recommendations

Choosing the Right Option:

  • Rental Car: Best if you want to explore volcano, rainforest, and entire island independently. Most flexibility.
  • Public Buses + Walking: Good for budget travelers staying in Island City/beach areas only.
  • Taxis + Hotel Shuttle: Convenient for those not wanting to drive but costs add up.
  • Bikes: Fun for fit travelers in flat city areas, but impractical for distant attractions.

Driving Tips:

  • Drive on the right side of the road (same as US)
  • Speed limits: 25 mph in city, 45 mph on highways
  • Parking: Free parking at most beaches and attractions. Metered parking in downtown Island City.
  • Road conditions: Well-maintained main roads. Some rural/mountain roads may be narrow.

Safety & Practical Info:

  • Traffic is generally light except during rush hours (7-9am, 4-6pm) in Island City
  • Gas stations close around 10pm - plan accordingly
  • Cell phone coverage good in populated areas, limited in mountains
  • GPS/maps apps work well for navigation
